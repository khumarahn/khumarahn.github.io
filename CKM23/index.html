Superdiffusive limits beyond the Marcus regime for deterministic fast-slow systems

Ilya Chevyrev, Alexey Korepanov, Ian Melbourne

Consider Birkhoff sums \(S_n = \sum_{k < n} v \circ T^k \) where \(T \colon \Lambda \to \Lambda\) is a slowly mixing dynamical system and \(v \colon M \to \mathbb{R}^d\) is a smooth observable with average \(0\). For example, \(T\) is the collision map for a billiard with a flat cusp as in Jung and Zhang arXiv:1611.00879, \( v(r, \varphi) = (\cos (3 \varphi), \cos(5 \varphi) ) \) with \(d = 2\). (Here \(\varphi\) is the post-collisional angle between particle's velocity and the normal to the boundary, and \(r\) is the arclength along the boundary.)

Choosing a random initial condition and a large \(n \geq 1\), we investigate properties of the process \[ W_n(t) = \frac{1}{n^{2/3}} S_{\lfloor n t \rfloor} . \] In particular, we are interested in solutions of differential equations of the type \[dX = A(X) \, dt + B(X) \, dW_n .\]
